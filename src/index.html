Welcome to the 2014 Boston Garbage Atlas

Eh -it's hardly an atlas, actually. It's an entry into the 2017 Analyze Boston Open Data Challenge. Mapped here (temporally and spatially) are the Big Bellies' calls (for help?) during and around four specific events in 2014. While no attempt was made at analysis (e.g. do Wine Rioters generate more garbage than Marathon fans?), one could, feasibly, do so. But why? Garbage is beautiful as-is.



Select an event with the tabs, above, and Belly alerts for that event's timespan (represented by the slider up there) will begin lighting up with the color of their alert status (green for "I'm aight"; yellow for "I can't take much more"; red for "somebody please evacuate me"; white/brown [depending on the map style] for Bellies yet to make a call within the event's temporal range)

ABOUT

Available from Boston Open Data are Big Belly Alerts for the entirety of 2014. These are signals sent by, let's be honest, trash cans that indicate their remaining capacity. Here you will find a few select subsets of 2014 data that coincide with various events in the city that year.
